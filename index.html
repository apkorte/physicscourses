Frequently asked questions

Physics Revision — Common Questions

What is an A-level Physics revision course?

An A-level Physics revision course is an intensive programme designed to consolidate understanding and exam technique ahead of A-level examinations. Dr Korte's courses cover the full syllabus in small online groups of no more than six students, allowing targeted support and rapid progress.

Which exam boards do you cover?

Courses cover all major UK A-level Physics exam boards: AQA, OCR A, OCR B (Advancing Physics), Edexcel, and WJEC Eduqas.

How does online Physics tuition work?

Sessions run live via Zoom in small groups. Dr Korte teaches using shared digital materials, works through problems in real time, and tailors explanations to the questions that arise. Students need only a device with internet access.

When are the Easter and summer Physics revision courses?

Easter intensive courses run during the Easter school holiday period, typically in April. Summer intensive courses run in June and July ahead of resit or Year 12 progression. Christmas intensives are also available. Exact dates and booking are at physicsandmathscourses.com.

What makes these Physics courses different?

Dr Korte holds a First Class degree in Physics from Oxford, an MSc with Distinction in Mathematical Modelling from UCL, and a PhD in Theoretical Physics from Imperial College London. Courses are built around deep subject understanding rather than surface exam technique, giving students the foundation to answer any question with confidence.

Is the course suitable for Year 12 and Year 13?

Yes. Courses are available for both Year 12 students consolidating their first year and Year 13 students preparing for final A-level examinations. Course content is tailored to the specific year group and exam board of each cohort.
